Stan Lee

The man who brought superheroes to life

Publishing career

  • 1939 - Became an assistant at the new Timely Comics division belonging to pulp magazine and comic-book publisher Martin Goodman.
  • 1950 - Teamed up with Dan DeCarlo to produce the syndicated newspaper strip My Friend Irma, based on the radio comedy starring Marie Wilson.
  • 1960 - Scripted, art-directed and edited most of Marvel's series, moderated the letters pages, wrote a monthly column called "Stan's Soapbox".
  • 1972 - Stopped writing monthly comic books to assume the role of publisher.
